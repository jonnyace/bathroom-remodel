Quote-ready scope notes

  • Demo: remove existing vanity/toilet finishes as needed; inspect subfloor and framing.
  • Plumbing: relocate toilet to left/opposite wall; verify drain size, slope, venting, joist path.
  • Shower: choose pan system, waterproofing membrane, curb/curbless, drain location, glass/opening.
  • Electrical: GFCI outlets, vanity lighting, shower-rated light if inside wet zone, exhaust fan.
  • HVAC: relocate/register left wall without conflicting with toilet rough-in.
  • Carpentry: blocking for live-edge shelf, shower glass, accessories, future grab bars.

Contractor questions

  • Can the toilet drain/vent legally move to the left wall without ugly soffits or floor surgery?
  • Where are the joists running under the proposed shower drain?
  • What waterproofing system will you warranty, and where does it terminate?
  • Can the left 29" door safely swing out, and does the right 29" door swing inward away from the sink without hitting shower entry/circulation?
  • Will the HVAC register location interfere with toilet clearance or wall blocking?
This is an owner-planning schematic. Have licensed trades/GC verify plumbing, electrical, HVAC, waterproofing, clearances, structure, and local code before building.
