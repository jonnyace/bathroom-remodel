Quote notes

QUOTE-READY SCOPE NOTES

  • Demo: remove existing vanity/toilet finishes as needed; inspect subfloor and framing.
  • Plumbing: relocate toilet to left wall; verify drain size, slope, venting, joist path.
  • Sink: wall-mount high-back farm sink with two-hole splash faucet, set into/cut around a full-width live-edge counter; verify sink hanger/rim height, supply/drain rough-ins, wall blocking, and counter support.
  • Shower: doorless walk-in with open right-side entry away from the toilet, half wall on the toilet side, glass to ceiling, shower head on the same left wall as the toilet, pan/waterproofing, curb/curbless, drain location.
  • Electrical: GFCI outlets, vanity lighting, shower-rated light if inside wet zone, exhaust fan.
  • HVAC: register to opposite/right wall across from toilet; verify duct path and framing conflicts.
  • Carpentry: solid blocking for the wall-hung sink and live-edge counter, shower glass, accessories, future grab bars.

ASK THE CONTRACTOR

  1. Can the toilet drain/vent legally move to the left wall without soffits or floor surgery?
  2. Where are the joists running under the proposed shower drain?
  3. Can the shower head and valve be roughed into the same left wall as the toilet without fighting toilet plumbing/venting?
  4. What waterproofing system will you warranty around the half wall, glass channel, open entry, and shower-head wall penetrations?
  5. Can the wall-mount high-back sink and full-width live-edge counter both be supported cleanly with blocking/brackets?
  6. Can the left 29″ door safely swing out, and does the right 29″ door swing inward away from the sink without hitting circulation?
  7. Can the AC register move to the right wall without duct, framing, or shower-glass conflicts?

RISK FLAGS / PROFESSIONAL VERIFICATION

  • Moving the toilet is expensive and venting must be legal.
  • Shower leaks ruin houses: get the waterproofing system and glass/half-wall transitions warranted.
  • Wall-hung farm sink plus live-edge counter needs real blocking/support, not drywall anchors. Obviously.
  • GFCI everywhere wet; shower-rated light if inside wet zone.
  • Finished dimensions must be rechecked after wall board, tile, trim, and door casing.
OWNER-PLANNING SCHEMATIC. Have licensed trades / GC verify plumbing, electrical, HVAC, waterproofing, clearances, structure, and local code before building. Finished dimensions to be verified after wall board, tile, and trim.
