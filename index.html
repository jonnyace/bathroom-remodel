Known room size roughly 66″ wide × 132″ long. Current direction: a single live-edge floating shelf with one basin between two 29″ doors (left door swings out, right door swings in away from the sink), the toilet relocated to the left wall, an HVAC register on the opposite/right wall across from the toilet, and a doorless walk-in shower at the old toilet end — half wall on the toilet side, fixed glass from the half wall up to the ceiling, open entry on the right side away from the toilet, with the shower head moved to the same left wall as the toilet. All dimensions are concept-level until field verified.

FIELD VERIFY

  • Exact locations + hinge sides of both 29″ doors flanking the sink
  • Left door outswing clearance; right door inswing path away from sink
  • Joist direction under proposed shower drain
  • Toilet drain rough-in target + legal vent route
  • Shower finished dims, entry width, half-wall length/height, glass-to-ceiling detail
  • Shower head + valve rough-in on the same left wall as the toilet
  • HVAC boot size + duct bay for right-wall register
  • Ceiling height, exhaust fan path, light/switch locations

RISK FLAGS

  • Moving the toilet is expensive
  • Venting must be legal — no ugly soffits or floor surgery
  • Shower leaks ruin houses: warranty the waterproofing system
  • GFCI everywhere wet; shower-rated light if inside wet zone

Quote-ready scope & contractor questions

QUOTE-READY SCOPE NOTES

  • Demo: remove existing vanity/toilet finishes as needed; inspect subfloor and framing.
  • Plumbing: relocate toilet to left wall; verify drain size, slope, venting, joist path.
  • Shower: doorless walk-in with open right-side entry away from the toilet, half wall on toilet side, glass to ceiling, shower head/valve on the left/toilet wall, pan/waterproofing, curb/curbless, drain location.
  • Electrical: GFCI outlets, vanity lighting, shower-rated light if inside wet zone, exhaust fan.
  • HVAC: register to opposite/right wall across from toilet; verify duct path and framing conflicts.
  • Carpentry: blocking for live-edge shelf, shower glass, accessories, future grab bars.

ASK THE CONTRACTOR

  1. Can the toilet drain/vent legally move to the left wall without soffits or floor surgery?
  2. Can the shower head and valve be roughed into the same left wall as the toilet without fighting the toilet plumbing/venting?
  3. Where are the joists running under the proposed shower drain?
  4. What waterproofing system will you warranty around the half wall, glass channel, open entry, and shower-head wall penetrations?
  5. Can the left 29″ door safely swing out, and does the right 29″ door swing inward away from the sink without hitting circulation?
  6. Can the AC register move to the right wall without duct, framing, or shower-glass conflicts?
OWNER-PLANNING SCHEMATIC. Have licensed trades / GC verify plumbing, electrical, HVAC, waterproofing, clearances, structure, and local code before building. Finished dimensions to be verified after wall board, tile, and trim.
