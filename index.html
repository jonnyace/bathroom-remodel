Build notes by trade

  • Plumbing: toilet move means waste line and venting are the big-ticket unknowns. Confirm joist direction before assuming the route is easy.
  • Shower: pick waterproofing system early — foam pan, mud bed, or sheet membrane. The drain choice controls slope and framing details.
  • HVAC: left-wall register still works, but verify it does not crowd the new toilet centerline/clearance envelope.
  • Electrical: vanity outlets need GFCI protection; lighting boxes should be placed after mirror width is chosen.
  • Framing: add blocking now for floating counter, shower glass, grab-bar futureproofing, and accessories.

Most likely “gotchas”

  • Outswing door needing hall-side clearance, casing clearance, and code/egress sanity check.
  • Shower drain landing on a joist or awkward framing member.
  • Register boot or duct crowding the opposite-wall toilet rough-in.
  • Floating live-edge shelf ending too high if the basin is a vessel sink.
  • Forgetting finished-wall thickness when using the 66 7/16" bare-stud measurement.
